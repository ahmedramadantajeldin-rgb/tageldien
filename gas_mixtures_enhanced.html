Single Gas Laws — مراجعة قوانين الغاز الواحد

قبل الدخول في مخاليط الغازات، نراجع قوانين الغاز المثالي الواحد بصورتيه: Mass-based form وMole-based form.

الكميةMass-Based Form (per kg)Mole-Based Form (per kmol)
Ideal Gas Law$PV = mRT$$PV = N\bar{R}T$
Specific Volume$Pv = RT\;,\quad v=V/m$$P\bar{v}=\bar{R}T\;,\quad\bar{v}=V/N$
Internal Energy$u = C_v T$$\bar{u}=\bar{C}_v T$
Enthalpy$h = C_p T$$\bar{h}=\bar{C}_p T$
Entropy Change$\Delta S = C_v\ln\dfrac{T_2}{T_1}+R\ln\dfrac{v_2}{v_1}$$\Delta\bar{S}=\bar{C}_v\ln\dfrac{T_2}{T_1}+\bar{R}\ln\dfrac{\bar{v}_2}{\bar{v}_1}$
Specific Heat Ratio$\gamma = k = C_p/C_v = \bar{C}_p/\bar{C}_v$
⚠ توحيد الرموز — Symbol Unification
R (بدون bar) = Specific Gas Constant — kJ/kg·K — تختلف من غاز لآخر
R̄ (بـ bar) = Universal Gas Constant = 8.314 kJ/kmol·K — ثابت لجميع الغازات
العلاقة: $R = \bar{R}/M$ حيث $M$ هي الـ Molar Mass بوحدة kg/kmol
📐 اشتقاق R من R̄
$$N\bar{R}T = mRT \;\Rightarrow\; R = \frac{N\bar{R}}{m} = \frac{\bar{R}}{M}$$
Example: O₂

$M_{O_2} = 16\times2 = 32\ \text{kg/kmol}$

$R_{O_2} = 8.314/32 = 0.2598\ \text{kJ/kg·K}$

Example: N₂

$M_{N_2} = 14\times2 = 28\ \text{kg/kmol}$

$R_{N_2} = 8.314/28 = 0.2969\ \text{kJ/kg·K}$

Internal Energy, Enthalpy & Entropy — المراجعة العميقة

Internal Energy — الطاقة الداخلية

للغاز المثالي تعتمد على درجة الحرارة فقط: $u = C_v T\;,\quad \bar{u} = \bar{C}_v T$

Enthalpy — الإنثالبي

$$h = u + Pv = C_pT \qquad \bar{h}=\bar{u}+P\bar{v}=\bar{C}_vT+\bar{R}T=\bar{C}_pT$$ $$\boxed{C_p = C_v + R} \qquad \boxed{\bar{C}_p = \bar{C}_v + \bar{R}}$$
Derivation — §0c

Specific Heat Ratio γ (k) — الاشتقاق والقيم

Monatomic
He, Ar, Ne
γ = 1.667
3 degrees of freedom
Diatomic
N₂, O₂, H₂
γ = 1.4
5 degrees of freedom
Polyatomic
CO₂, CH₄, H₂O
γ = 1.33
6 degrees of freedom
📐 اشتقاق C̄ₚ و C̄ᵥ من γ
$$\gamma\bar{C}_v = \bar{C}_v+\bar{R} \;\Rightarrow\; \bar{C}_v(\gamma-1)=\bar{R}$$ $$\boxed{\bar{C}_v=\frac{\bar{R}}{\gamma-1}} \qquad \boxed{\bar{C}_p=\frac{\gamma\bar{R}}{\gamma-1}} \qquad \boxed{C_v=\frac{R}{\gamma-1}} \qquad \boxed{C_p=\frac{\gamma R}{\gamma-1}}$$
📐 اشتقاق Entropy من القانون الأول
$$dq = C_v\,dT+P\,dv \;\Rightarrow\; ds=\frac{dq}{T}=\frac{C_v dT}{T}+\frac{R\,dv}{v}$$ $$\boxed{\Delta S = C_v\ln\frac{T_2}{T_1}+R\ln\frac{v_2}{v_1}} \qquad \boxed{\Delta\bar{S}=\bar{C}_v\ln\frac{T_2}{T_1}+\bar{R}\ln\frac{\bar{v}_2}{\bar{v}_1}}$$

Section 13–1

تركيب المخلوط — Composition of a Gas Mixture

Figure 13–1 — Mass of mixture = sum of component masses.
Figure 13–2 — Moles of mixture = sum of component moles.
Eq. 13–1 $$m_m = \sum_{i=1}^{k} m_i \qquad N_m = \sum_{i=1}^{k} N_i$$

A) Mass Fraction — Xi (also: mfi)

⚠ توحيد الرموز
X_i في محاضرات الدكتور = mf_i في الكتاب — نفس الكمية تماماً
Eq. 13–2a $$X_i \equiv mf_i = \frac{m_i}{m_{mix}} \qquad \sum_{i=1}^{k} X_i = 1$$

B) Mole Fraction — yi

Eq. 13–2b $$y_i = \frac{N_i}{N_{mix}} \qquad \sum_{i=1}^{k} y_i = 1$$
Figure 13–3 — Sum of mole fractions = 1.

Conversion: X ↔ y

Eq. 13–5 $$X_i = y_i\frac{M_i}{M_{mix}} \qquad\longleftrightarrow\qquad y_i = \frac{X_i/M_i}{\displaystyle\sum_j X_j/M_j}$$
Section 13–1 (cont.)

C & D) Apparent Molar Mass & Mixture Gas Constant

Eqs. 13–3a & 13–4 $$M_{mix} = \frac{m_{mix}}{N_{mix}} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} y_i M_i = \frac{1}{\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{k} X_i/M_i}$$
Eq. 13–3b $$R_{mix} = \frac{\bar{R}}{M_{mix}} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} X_i \cdot R_i$$
مثال — الهواء (O₂ 21% + N₂ 79%):
$M_{air}=(0.21)(32)+(0.79)(28)=28.84\ \text{kg/kmol}$
$R_{air}=8.314/28.84=0.2882\ \text{kJ/kg·K}$
Section 13–2

سلوك P-v-T — Dalton's & Amagat's Laws

Dalton's Law — الضغوط التجميعية

ضغط المخلوط = مجموع الضغوط عند نفس T و V لكل غاز على حدة.

Amagat's Law — الحجوم التجميعية

حجم المخلوط = مجموع الحجوم عند نفس T و P لكل غاز على حدة.

Figure 13–5 — Dalton's law for two ideal gases.
Figure 13–6 — Amagat's law for two ideal gases.
Dalton's Law — Eq. 13–6
$$P_m = \sum_{i=1}^{k} P_i(T_m, V_m)$$
Amagat's Law — Eq. 13–7
$$V_m = \sum_{i=1}^{k} V_i(T_m, P_m)$$
Section 13–2

العلاقة الجوهرية للمخاليط المثالية

Key Relation — Eq. 13–8 (Ideal Gas Mixtures Only)
$$\boxed{\frac{P_i}{P_m} = \frac{V_i}{V_m} = \frac{N_i}{N_m} = y_i}$$

في المخلوط المثالي: الـmole fraction = الـpressure fraction = الـvolume fraction. الـpartial pressure: $P_i = y_i P_m$

Figure 13–7 — Component volumes for O₂ and N₂ at same T and P.
📐 اشتقاق Partial Pressure
$$P_{mix}=\sum P_i=\sum\frac{N_i\bar{R}T}{V}=\frac{\bar{R}T}{V}\sum N_i \;\Rightarrow\; \boxed{P_i = y_i P_{mix}} \quad\text{and}\quad P_iV_i=N_i\bar{R}T$$
ملاحظة: في المخاليط المثالية فقط تتطابق الـcomponent pressure مع الـpartial pressure.
Section 13–2

Real Gas Mixtures — المخاليط الحقيقية

المنهج الأول: Compressibility Factor

Eq. 13–10 $$Z_m = \sum_{i=1}^{k} y_i Z_i \qquad P_mV_m = Z_m N_m\bar{R}T_m$$

المنهج الثاني: Kay's Rule

Eq. 13–11 $$P'_{cr,m} = \sum y_i P_{cr,i} \qquad T'_{cr,m} = \sum y_i T_{cr,i}$$ $$T_R = T_m/T'_{cr,m} \qquad P_R = P_m/P'_{cr,m} \xrightarrow{\text{chart}} Z_m$$
Amagat vs Dalton: Amagat (عند $T_m$, $P_m$) أدق للضغوط العالية. Dalton أنسب للضغوط المنخفضة.
Section 13–3

Mixture Properties — Enthalpy, Cp, Cv, γ

Eqs. 13–19 to 13–23 $$h_{mix} = \sum X_i\, h_i \qquad u_{mix} = \sum X_i\, u_i$$ $$c_{p,mix} = \sum X_i\, c_{p,i} \qquad c_{v,mix} = \sum X_i\, c_{v,i}$$ $$\gamma_{mix} = \frac{c_{p,mix}}{c_{v,mix}} = \frac{\sum X_i\, c_{p,i}}{\sum X_i\, c_{v,i}}$$
قاعدة: خصائص لكل kg → تستخدم mass fraction $X_i$. خصائص لكل kmol → تستخدم mole fraction $y_i$.
Section 13–3

Entropy Change — Partial Pressure

تنص Gibbs-Dalton law على أن كل غاز يتصرف بضغطه الجزئي. التغير في Entropy:

Eq. 13–24 $$\Delta s_i \approx c_{p,i}\ln\frac{T_2}{T_1} - R_i\ln\frac{P_{i,2}}{P_{i,1}} \qquad\text{where}\; P_{i} = y_i P_m$$
Mixture Entropy $$\Delta S_{mix} = \sum m_i\,\Delta s_i = m_{mix}\left[c_{v,mix}\ln\frac{T_2}{T_1}+R_{mix}\ln\frac{V_2}{V_1}\right]$$
تنبيه حاسم: في حساب ΔS دائماً يُستخدم الـpartial pressure للمكون، وليس الضغط الكلي.

1
A new refrigerant, R-407, is a mixture of 23% R-32, 25% R-125, and 52% R-134a on a mass basis. Find the mole fractions, the mixture gas constant, and the mixture heat capacities.
Given
Mass fractions: mf(R-32)=0.23, mf(R-125)=0.25, mf(R-134a)=0.52
Molar masses: M(R-32)=52 | M(R-125)=120 | M(R-134a)=102 kg/kmol
Cp: R-32=0.823, R-125=0.791, R-134a=0.852 kJ/kg·K   Cv: R-32=0.497, R-125=0.714, R-134a=0.791 kJ/kg·K
Required
Mole fractions yi  |  Rmix  |  Cp,mix and Cv,mix
Solution
1
Moles per 100 kg basis:
\[ N_1=\frac{23}{52}=0.4423\ \text{kmol},\quad N_2=\frac{25}{120}=0.2083\ \text{kmol},\quad N_3=\frac{52}{102}=0.5098\ \text{kmol} \] \[ N_{tot}=1.1604\ \text{kmol} \]
2
Mole fractions:
\[ y_1=\frac{0.4423}{1.1604}=0.381,\quad y_2=\frac{0.2083}{1.1604}=0.180,\quad y_3=\frac{0.5098}{1.1604}=0.439 \]
Check: 0.381+0.180+0.439 = 1.000 ✓
3
Mixture molar mass and gas constant:
\[ M_{mix}=0.381(52)+0.180(120)+0.439(102)=86.2\ \text{kg/kmol} \] \[ R_{mix}=\frac{8.314}{86.2}=0.0965\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \]
4
Mixture specific heats:
\[ C_{p,mix}=0.23(0.823)+0.25(0.791)+0.52(0.852)=0.189+0.198+0.443=0.830\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ C_{v,mix}=0.23(0.497)+0.25(0.714)+0.52(0.791)=0.114+0.179+0.411=0.704\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \]
Final Answers
y₁(R-32)=38.1%y₂(R-125)=18.0%y₃(R-134a)=43.9% Mmix=86.2 kg/kmolRmix=0.0965 kJ/kg·K Cp,mix=0.830 kJ/kg·KCv,mix=0.704 kJ/kg·K

A new refrigerant, R-410a, is a mixture of R-32 and R-125 in a 1:1 mass ratio. What are the overall molecular weight, the gas constant, and the ratio of specific heats?
Given
mf(R-32)=0.5, mf(R-125)=0.5   M(R-32)=52, M(R-125)=120 kg/kmol
Cp: R-32=0.823, R-125=0.791 kJ/kg·K   Cv: R-32=0.497, R-125=0.714 kJ/kg·K
Required
Mmix, Rmix, γmix
Solution
1
Moles and mole fractions (100 kg basis):
\[ N_1=\frac{50}{52}=0.9615\ \text{kmol},\quad N_2=\frac{50}{120}=0.4167\ \text{kmol},\quad N_{tot}=1.3782\ \text{kmol} \] \[ y_{R-32}=\frac{0.9615}{1.3782}=0.698,\quad y_{R-125}=\frac{0.4167}{1.3782}=0.302 \]
2
Molar mass and gas constant:
\[ M_{mix}=\frac{100}{1.3782}=72.56\ \text{kg/kmol} \] \[ R_{mix}=\frac{8.314}{72.56}=0.1146\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \]
3
Specific heats and ratio γ:
\[ C_{p,mix}=0.5(0.823)+0.5(0.791)=0.807\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ C_{v,mix}=0.5(0.497)+0.5(0.714)=0.606\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ \gamma_{mix}=\frac{0.807}{0.606}=1.332 \]
Final Answers
Mmix=72.56 kg/kmolRmix=0.1146 kJ/kg·Kγmix=1.332
3
A flow of 1 kg/s argon at 300 K and another flow of 1 kg/s CO₂ at 1600 K, both at 150 kPa, are mixed without heat transfer. Find the exit temperature and pressure.
Given
ṁAr=1 kg/s, TAr=300 K   ṁCO₂=1 kg/s, TCO₂=1600 K   P=150 kPa (both)   Adiabatic
Cp,Ar=0.5203, Cp,CO₂=0.846 kJ/kg·K
Required
Exit T₃ and P₃
Solution
1
Adiabatic energy balance — steady-flow mixing:
\[ T_3=\frac{\dot{m}_{Ar}C_{p,Ar}T_{Ar}+\dot{m}_{CO_2}C_{p,CO_2}T_{CO_2}}{\dot{m}_{Ar}C_{p,Ar}+\dot{m}_{CO_2}C_{p,CO_2}}=\frac{1(0.5203)(300)+1(0.846)(1600)}{0.5203+0.846}=\frac{156.1+1353.6}{1.3663}\approx1103\ \text{K} \]
2
Exit pressure: Both inlets at same P → P₃ = 150 kPa
Final Answers
T₃ ≈ 1103 KP₃ = 150 kPa
4
An insulated gas turbine receives a mixture of 10% CO₂, 10% H₂O, and 80% N₂ by mass at 1000 K, 500 kPa. Volume flow rate V̇=2 m³/s. Exhaust at 700 K, 100 kPa. Find the power output.
Given
mfCO₂=0.10, mfH₂O=0.10, mfN₂=0.80   T₁=1000 K, P₁=500 kPa, V̇=2 m³/s
T₂=700 K, P₂=100 kPa   Cp: CO₂=0.846, H₂O=1.872, N₂=1.039 kJ/kg·K
Required
Power output Ẇ (kW)
Solution
1
Mixture Cp:
\[ C_{p,mix}=0.10(0.846)+0.10(1.872)+0.80(1.039)=1.103\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \]
2
Mmix and Rmix (100 kg basis):
\[ N_{tot}=\tfrac{10}{44}+\tfrac{10}{18}+\tfrac{80}{28}=3.636\ \text{kmol} \] \[ M_{mix}=100/3.636=27.50\ \text{kg/kmol},\quad R_{mix}=8.314/27.50=0.3023\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \]
3
Mass flow rate and power:
\[ \dot{m}=\frac{P_1\dot{V}}{R_{mix}T_1}=\frac{500\times2}{0.3023\times1000}=3.308\ \text{kg/s} \] \[ \dot{W}=\dot{m}\,C_{p,mix}(T_1-T_2)=3.308\times1.103\times300=1094\ \text{kW} \]
Final Answers
Ẇ ≈ 1094 kW
5
A mixture of 0.5 kg N₂ and 0.5 kg O₂ at 100 kPa, 300 K in a constant-pressure piston/cylinder. 800 kJ is added by heating. Find the final temperature and entropy increase.
Given
mN₂=0.5 kg, mO₂=0.5 kg   P=100 kPa (const.)   T₁=300 K   Q=800 kJ
Cp,N₂=1.039, Cp,O₂=0.922 kJ/kg·K
Required
T₂ and ΔSmix
Solution
1
Mixture Cp and final temperature:
\[ C_{p,mix}=0.5(1.039)+0.5(0.922)=0.9805\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ T_2=300+\frac{Q}{m_{mix}C_{p,mix}}=300+\frac{800}{1.0\times0.9805}=1116\ \text{K} \]
2
Entropy change (const. P → ΔS = mCpln(T₂/T₁)):
\[ \Delta S_{N_2}=0.5(1.039)\ln\frac{1116}{300}=0.682\ \text{kJ/K},\quad \Delta S_{O_2}=0.5(0.922)\ln\frac{1116}{300}=0.605\ \text{kJ/K} \] \[ \Delta S_{mix}=0.682+0.605=1.287\ \text{kJ/K} \]
Final Answers
T₂ ≈ 1116 KΔSmix = 1.287 kJ/K
6
A steady flow of 0.1 kmol/s CO₂ at 1000 K is mixed with 0.2 kmol/s N₂ at 400 K at 100 kPa, then isentropically compressed to 500 kPa. Find the mixing chamber exit temperature and the compressor power.
Given
ṄCO₂=0.1 kmol/s at TCO₂=1000 K   ṄN₂=0.2 kmol/s at TN₂=400 K
P₁=100 kPa → P₂=500 kPa (isentropic)   C̄p,CO₂=37.1, C̄p,N₂=29.1 kJ/kmol·K
Required
(a) Mixing exit temperature T₃   (b) Compressor power Ẇc
Solution
1
Mole fractions and mixture C̄p:
\[ \dot{N}_{mix}=0.1+0.2=0.3\ \text{kmol/s},\quad y_{CO_2}=\frac{1}{3}=0.333,\quad y_{N_2}=\frac{2}{3}=0.667 \] \[ \bar{C}_{p,mix}=\frac{1}{3}(37.1)+\frac{2}{3}(29.1)=12.367+19.400=31.77\ \text{kJ/kmol·K} \]
2
Mixing chamber — adiabatic energy balance:
\[ T_3=\frac{\dot{N}_{CO_2}\bar{C}_{p,CO_2}T_{CO_2}+\dot{N}_{N_2}\bar{C}_{p,N_2}T_{N_2}}{\dot{N}_{mix}\bar{C}_{p,mix}}=\frac{0.1(37.1)(1000)+0.2(29.1)(400)}{0.3(31.77)} \] \[ T_3=\frac{3710+2328}{9.531}=633.5\ \text{K} \]
3
Isentropic compression — find γmix and T₄:
\[ \bar{C}_{v,mix}=\bar{C}_{p,mix}-\bar{R}=31.77-8.314=23.46\ \text{kJ/kmol·K} \] \[ \gamma_{mix}=\frac{31.77}{23.46}=1.354,\qquad\frac{\gamma-1}{\gamma}=\frac{0.354}{1.354}=0.2615 \] \[ T_4=T_3\left(\frac{P_2}{P_1}\right)^{(\gamma-1)/\gamma}=633.5\times(5)^{0.2615}=633.5\times1.523=965\ \text{K} \]
4
Compressor power:
\[ \dot{W}_c=\dot{N}_{mix}\bar{C}_{p,mix}(T_4-T_3)=0.3\times31.77\times(965-633.5)=0.3\times31.77\times331.5=3161\ \text{kW} \]
Final Answers
T₃ = 633.5 KT₄ = 965 KẆc ≈ 3161 kW
7
CO₂ gas at 320 K is mixed with nitrogen at 280 K in an insulated mixing chamber. Both flows at 100 kPa. Mole ratio CO₂:N₂ = 2:1. Find exit temperature and entropy generation per kmol of exit mixture.
Given
TCO₂=320 K, TN₂=280 K   P=100 kPa   NCO₂:NN₂=2:1
C̄p,CO₂=37.1, C̄p,N₂=29.1 kJ/kmol·K
Required
(a) Exit T₃   (b) σgen per kmol of mixture
Solution
1
Basis: 2 kmol CO₂ + 1 kmol N₂ → 3 kmol mixture:
\[ y_{CO_2}=\tfrac{2}{3},\quad y_{N_2}=\tfrac{1}{3},\quad \bar{C}_{p,mix}=\tfrac{2}{3}(37.1)+\tfrac{1}{3}(29.1)=34.43\ \text{kJ/kmol·K} \]
2
Adiabatic mixing temperature:
\[ T_3=\frac{2(37.1)(320)+1(29.1)(280)}{3(34.43)}=\frac{23744+8148}{103.29}=308.7\ \text{K} \]
3
Entropy generation — using partial pressures (Gibbs-Dalton law):

Before mixing: each gas pure at 100 kPa. After mixing: $P_{CO_2}=\frac{2}{3}(100)=66.7$ kPa, $P_{N_2}=\frac{1}{3}(100)=33.3$ kPa

\[ \sigma_{CO_2}=37.1\ln\frac{308.7}{320}-8.314\ln\frac{66.7}{100}=-1.323+3.371=2.048\ \text{kJ/kmol·K} \] \[ \sigma_{N_2}=29.1\ln\frac{308.7}{280}-8.314\ln\frac{33.3}{100}=2.841+9.134=11.975\ \text{kJ/kmol·K} \] \[ \sigma_{gen}=\frac{2(2.048)+1(11.975)}{3}=\frac{16.071}{3}=5.36\ \text{kJ/kmol·K}>0\;\checkmark \]
Final Answers
T₃ ≈ 308.7 Kσgen ≈ 5.36 kJ/kmol·K
8
A gas mixture (15% N₂, 5% He, 60% CH₄, 20% C₂H₆ by mass) in a 4 m³ rigid insulated vessel at 150 kPa, 30°C. A paddle wheel does 200 kJ of work. Find the final pressure and temperature.
Given
mfN₂=0.15, mfHe=0.05, mfCH₄=0.60, mfC₂H₆=0.20
V=4 m³, P₁=150 kPa, T₁=303.15 K, insulated, Win=200 kJ
Cv: N₂=0.743, He=3.116, CH₄=1.736, C₂H₆=1.549 kJ/kg·K
Required
Final T₂ and P₂
Solution
1
Mixture Cv, Mmix and Rmix:
\[ C_{v,mix}=0.15(0.743)+0.05(3.116)+0.60(1.736)+0.20(1.549)=1.619\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ N_{tot}=\tfrac{15}{28}+\tfrac{5}{4}+\tfrac{60}{16}+\tfrac{20}{30}=6.203\ \text{kmol} \] \[ M_{mix}=100/6.203=16.12\ \text{kg/kmol},\quad R_{mix}=8.314/16.12=0.5157\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \]
2
Mass in vessel and final temperature:
\[ m=\frac{P_1V}{R_{mix}T_1}=\frac{150\times4}{0.5157\times303.15}=3.836\ \text{kg} \] \[ T_2=T_1+\frac{W_{in}}{mC_{v,mix}}=303.15+\frac{200}{3.836\times1.619}=335.3\ \text{K} \]
3
Final pressure (V = const → P ∝ T):
\[ P_2=P_1\frac{T_2}{T_1}=150\times\frac{335.3}{303.15}=165.9\ \text{kPa} \]
Final Answers
T₂ ≈ 335.3 K (62.2°C)P₂ ≈ 165.9 kPa
9
An insulated tank (1 kg O₂ at 15°C, 300 kPa) is connected to a 2-m³ uninsulated tank (N₂ at 50°C, 500 kPa). Valve opened → mixture at 25°C. Find (a) final P, (b) heat transfer Q, (c) entropy generated Sgen. T₀=25°C.
Given
Tank A: mO₂=1 kg, T=288.15 K, P=300 kPa  |  Tank B: VN₂=2 m³, T=323.15 K, P=500 kPa
Tf=298.15 K   RO₂=0.2598, RN₂=0.2969 kJ/kg·K   Cv,O₂=0.6475, Cv,N₂=0.743 kJ/kg·K
Required
(a) Pf   (b) Q   (c) Sgen
Solution
1
Initial volumes and masses:
\[ V_{O_2}=\frac{1\times0.2598\times288.15}{300}=0.2497\ \text{m}^3,\quad m_{N_2}=\frac{500\times2}{0.2969\times323.15}=10.43\ \text{kg} \]
Vtotal=2.2497 m³, mmix=11.43 kg
2
Mixture properties and final pressure:
\[ x_{O_2}=0.0875,\quad x_{N_2}=0.9125,\quad R_{mix}=0.0875(0.2598)+0.9125(0.2969)=0.2937\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ P_f=\frac{m_{mix}R_{mix}T_f}{V_{total}}=\frac{11.43\times0.2937\times298.15}{2.2497}=444.6\ \text{kPa} \]
3
Heat transfer — 1st Law rigid system:
\[ Q=m_{O_2}C_{v,O_2}(T_f-T_{O_2})+m_{N_2}C_{v,N_2}(T_f-T_{N_2}) \] \[ Q=1(0.6475)(10)+10.43(0.743)(-25)=6.475-193.8=-187.3\ \text{kJ (out)} \]
4
Entropy generation:
\[ \Delta S_{O_2}=1\left[0.6475\ln\frac{298.15}{288.15}+0.2598\ln\frac{2.2497}{0.2497}\right]=+0.5933\ \text{kJ/K} \] \[ \Delta S_{N_2}=10.43\left[0.743\ln\frac{298.15}{323.15}+0.2969\ln\frac{0.2157}{0.1918}\right]=-0.2566\ \text{kJ/K} \] \[ \Delta S_{surr}=\frac{+187.3}{298.15}=+0.6282\ \text{kJ/K} \] \[ S_{gen}=0.5933-0.2566+0.6282=0.965\ \text{kJ/K}>0\;\checkmark \]
Final Answers
(a) Pf ≈ 444.6 kPa(b) Q = −187.3 kJ(c) Sgen ≈ 0.965 kJ/K
10
An equimolar mixture of helium and argon is used as the working fluid in a closed-loop gas-turbine cycle. The mixture enters the turbine at 2.5 MPa, 1300 K and expands isentropically to 200 kPa. Find the work output per unit mass of mixture.
Given
yHe=yAr=0.5 (equimolar)   P₁=2500 kPa, T₁=1300 K, P₂=200 kPa
MHe=4, MAr=40 kg/kmol   Both monatomic → γ=1.667
Cp,He=5.193, Cp,Ar=0.5203 kJ/kg·K
Required
Turbine work output per unit mass wt (kJ/kg)
Solution
1
Mmix, Rmix and mass fractions:
\[ M_{mix}=0.5(4)+0.5(40)=22\ \text{kg/kmol},\quad R_{mix}=8.314/22=0.3779\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ mf_{He}=0.5\times\frac{4}{22}=0.0909,\quad mf_{Ar}=0.5\times\frac{40}{22}=0.9091 \]
2
Cp,mix, Cv,mix and γmix:
\[ C_{p,mix}=0.0909(5.193)+0.9091(0.5203)=0.945\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ C_{v,mix}=C_{p,mix}-R_{mix}=0.945-0.3779=0.567\ \text{kJ/kg·K} \] \[ \gamma_{mix}=0.945/0.567=1.667\quad\text{(same as monatomic — as expected)} \]
3
Isentropic exit temperature and work output:
\[ T_2=1300\left(\frac{200}{2500}\right)^{0.667/1.667}=1300\times(0.08)^{0.400}=473.3\ \text{K} \] \[ w_t=C_{p,mix}(T_1-T_2)=0.945\times(1300-473.3)=781.2\ \text{kJ/kg} \]
Final Answers
wt ≈ 781.2 kJ/kg
